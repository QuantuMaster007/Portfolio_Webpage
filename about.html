How I approach technical program management

7+ Years Experience

Who I Am

I am a Technical Program Manager focused on driving execution across complex, capital-intensive hardware programs in automotive and consumer electronics.

My work sits at the intersection of product development, engineering build readiness, capital expenditure strategy, CM / OEM execution, and global supply chain delivery. I partner closely with engineering, operations, finance, and external manufacturing partners to translate technical intent into build-ready, scalable outcomes.

I operate in environments where ambiguity is high, dependencies are tightly coupled, and decisions carry real cost, schedule, and capacity implications. With an MBA in Operations and MS in Business Analytics, I bring both financial acumen and data-driven rigor to cross-functional leadership.

How I Think About the TPM Role

I view technical program management as an execution discipline, not a coordination function.

My role is to create clarity where systems, teams, and constraints intersect — by defining ownership, readiness criteria, and decision paths early, and by maintaining operating cadence as programs evolve from concept through NPI to high-volume manufacturing.

I focus on surfacing risks and trade-offs early, so teams and leaders can make informed decisions before issues become costly or disruptive. This means driving CapEx planning with engineering milestones, ensuring tool installation readiness aligns with build schedules, and maintaining rigorous ECO governance across distributed manufacturing partners.
